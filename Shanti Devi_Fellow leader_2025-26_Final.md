**Case Study: Transformation through Leadership and Gender Trainings**

The participant (Shanti) grew up in a context of early hardship. Following the death of her father during childhood, the responsibility of raising the family shifted entirely to her mother, who worked as a daily wage laborer to sustain the household and ensure her children’s education. Despite severe financial constraints, the participant’s mother emphasized resilience and perseverance, shaping the participant’s early understanding of strength in adversity.

At a very young age, the participant was married through child marriage, before she had awareness of her rights or the opportunity to complete her education. Following marriage, she experienced prolonged emotional, physical, and psychological abuse within her marital home. Her mobility was restricted, decision-making power was minimal, and she faced repeated humiliation and deprivation. Over time, these circumstances significantly affected her confidence, sense of identity, and well-being.

A turning point occurred when community-based meetings facilitated under the programme began in her village. Initially hesitant due to social norms and fear, the participant gradually started attending these gatherings. Through interactions with trained facilitators and peer women leaders, she was exposed to discussions on gender equality, legal rights, and collective support mechanisms. These sessions helped her understand that the violence and discrimination she faced were not personal failures, but manifestations of broader gender inequalities.

Participation in leadership and gender training contributed to a gradual shift in her self-perception. The participant began speaking in group discussions, sharing her experiences, and learning about available legal and social support systems. Her confidence improved, and she began making decisions related to her children’s education and household matters.

The training also emphasized economic participation as a pathway to agency. Motivated by this learning, the participant became actively involved in farming. Agriculture evolved from a subsistence activity into a source of self-reliance and identity. This shift marked her transition from dependence toward economic participation.

Her exposure to collective spaces also strengthened her empathy-driven leadership. During this period, she took responsibility for caring for a newborn girl who had lost her mother. This decision reflected increased agency and the ability to take independent, compassionate action despite her own challenges.

Education became a priority within her household. The participant ensured that her children attended school, including residential schooling opportunities for girls. With encouragement from the group, she also resumed her own learning, reinforcing her confidence and long-term aspirations.

Currently, Shanti is actively engaged in community discussions and women’s group activities. She supports other women experiencing violence, encourages participation in collective platforms, and promotes children’s education within her village. Her mobility has improved, and she participates in meetings and programme-related activities beyond her household.

The Shanti’s journey illustrates how leadership and gender trainings can facilitate transformation from social isolation and low confidence to active community engagement, economic participation, and supportive leadership. The impact is visible not only at the individual level but also through intergenerational change, particularly in educational aspirations and community awareness.

Participation in leadership and gender trainings enabled the participant to transition from restricted mobility and low self-confidence to active community engagement and decision-making. She gained awareness of rights, resumed learning, and adopted farming as a livelihood activity.

She now supports children’s education and encourages other women to seek collective support. The change reflects strengthened agency, economic participation, and emerging grassroots leadership.
