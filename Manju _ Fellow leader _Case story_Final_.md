**From Survivor to Community Leader**

**“I refused to give up, for my children and my village.”**

Manju’s life story is not only about survival — it is about finding her voice and using it for change.

She grew up in hardship. After losing her father at a young age, her mother worked tirelessly to raise the family. Poverty was constant, but education was never abandoned. Her mother’s strength became her first lesson in resilience.

Yet, before she could complete her studies, Manju was married off at a very young age. She entered marriage without understanding her rights, responsibilities, or the life ahead. What followed were years of emotional, physical, and social hardship. Her mobility was restricted, decisions were made for her, and daily life was filled with humiliation and fear.

*“I thought this was my fate. I did not know that my life could be different.”*

A major turning point came when women’s meetings supported through the JAI AZADI programme/ AAS Meeting began in her village. At first, she was afraid to attend\_ She use to peep from her windows. But slowly, she gathered courage. There she met women who listened — truly listened — and facilitators who spoke about rights, equality, and self-respect.

Through leadership and gender trainings, Manju began to understand that violence was not destiny, and silence was not strength.

*“When I heard other women speak, I realized I was not alone. That day something changed inside me.”*

Her confidence slowly grew. She began participating in discussions, asking questions, and sharing her experiences. She restarted her own learning and made sure her children continued their education. She also started contributing to farming work, becoming more involved in decisions at home.

But Manju’s leadership did not stop at her doorstep.

**A Mother’s Fight for Education**

For nearly three years through interface meetings with officials at village, Block and District level, Manju fought for a school building in her village. Children had to walk long distances on unsafe roads just to study. Many dropped out. She could not accept that.

*“Every day I saw children walking so far. I used to think — if we don’t speak, who will?”*

She approached officials not once, but many times — nearly 8 to 10 visits. She used grievance systems, helplines like 1076 Jan sunvayi, and raised the issue in village spaces. She faced discouragement and doubt. Many told her nothing would change.

*“There were nights I cried, but the next morning I stood up again. I knew this fight was not only for my children, but for every child.”*

Her persistence paid off when the process for school construction finally began. This victory did not just bring a building — it brought dignity and recognition.

Today

Manju is no longer the woman who lived in fear. She speaks confidently in meetings, supports women facing violence, and encourages parents to educate their children. She travels outside her home from Bichia mihipurwa to Bahraich, Lucknow, Rajasthan(for exposure visits on NRM techniques) , participates in community discussions, and stands as a local example of courage.

*“I learned that we don’t have to stay silent. When women stand together, things change.”*

Her journey shows how leadership and gender training can transform not only an individual life but an entire community.

Manju’s story is not only about overcoming hardship —

it is about turning pain into purpose.

**Current Role and Impact**

Today, Manju is recognized as a grassroots woman leader. She supports other women experiencing violence, encourages participation in group meetings, and promotes children’s education. She moves freely, speaks confidently in public spaces, and participates in community decision-making.

Her journey shows how leadership and gender trainings can lead to:

1.  Increased self-confidence and voice
2.  Greater awareness of rights
3.  Economic participation
4.  Community-level leadership
5.  Education advocacy
6.  Collective action for local development

Manju’s story is no longer one of survival alone. It is a story of transformation — from silence to leadership — where personal empowerment has led to community change.
